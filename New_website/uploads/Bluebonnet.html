Texas teen licensing, explained

Getting a Texas license at 16 has a few required steps.

Here's the simple version — and we help with every one.

1

Get the learner permit

Complete the classroom portion and pass the written knowledge test to earn the learner license.

2

Behind-the-wheel training

In-car lessons with a certified instructor, plus supervised practice hours logged with a parent.

3

ITTD course

The short Impact Texas Teen Drivers video, required before the road test.

4

Pass the DPS road test

Which you can take right here with us — no long wait at the DPS office.

A note on requirements

Verify exact Texas hour requirements (classroom, behind-the-wheel, observation) and ITTD/permit rules against current TDLR/DPS guidance before enrolling — specifics change.

Home/FAQ
Questions & answers

Frequently asked questions.

The real answers to what parents and learners ask most. Don't see yours? Get in touch.

Getting started
What age can my teen start?
Texas teens can begin driver education at 14, with the learner permit typically available at 15. We'll guide you through the exact timing based on current DPS rules.
Do I need a permit first?
For behind-the-wheel lessons, yes — a learner license is required. The classroom portion helps your teen earn it. We'll tell you exactly what to do and when.
How do I enroll?
Pick a package on our Book a Lesson page and share your preferred times. We confirm within one business day.
Lessons
How many hours do I need to pass?
It varies by experience. Total beginners usually do best with our fuller packages, while drivers with some practice may only need a refresher. Texas sets minimum behind-the-wheel requirements for teen license-seekers — we'll make sure you meet them and feel genuinely ready.
Is it better to have 1- or 2-hour lessons?
For most learners, 2-hour lessons work best — there's time to warm up, practice a real skill, and build muscle memory without rushing. Nervous first-timers sometimes start shorter and build up.
Do you pick up?
Yes — we offer local pickup and drop-off within our service areas, so lessons happen on the roads you actually drive.
Testing & paperwork
Can I take my DPS road test with Bluebonnet?
Yes. As an approved provider we can administer the road skills test, so you avoid long waits at the DPS office. Ask us about scheduling your test with your package. (Verify this claim and current Texas requirements before publishing.)
What documents does DPS require?
It depends on your age and situation. Our Resources page lists the official DPS and TDLR forms, and we help students sort the paperwork every day.
What if I fail the test?
It happens, and it's okay. We'll review what to work on, get you more focused practice, and help you reschedule. Most students pass comfortably the next time.
